Give · Save · Fun

Every coin a child receives splits automatically by percentages the parent sets — and instantly gets a home of its own.

Money that belongs to someone else. The child picks a cause, watches the jar fill and then get donated — and remembers: the one who gave, not just the one who bought.

The ritual

How it works

Four simple actions — each one takes seconds.

01

Money comes in — any way

Cash from grandma, allowance, a chore, a birthday check — whatever your child receives, you log the amount in seconds.

02

Automatic split

Based on percentages set in advance (default: 10% · 10% · 80%) — the amount divides across the three jars.

03

The ritual

The child "taps" — an animation and a sound mimic a coin physically dropping into the jar.

04

Watch it in real time

The balance rises before their eyes. No waiting, no abstraction — the result is immediate.

You only log what comes in — not everyday spending. It takes seconds, not bookkeeping.

Where this is headed

A physical home for the money

The tap on a screen is a stand-in. We believe the fullest version of this ritual belongs in a child's hands — a box on their shelf, a coin they can hold before it disappears into a jar. That's the direction we're building toward, and testing with real families before we design the object itself.
